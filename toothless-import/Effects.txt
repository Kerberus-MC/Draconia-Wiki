# Blue Oleander

[TABLE]

This article is about the status effect. For the block, see Blue Oleander. For the item, see Blue Oleander Flower. For the Potion, see Concentrated Blue Oleander Poison.

Blue Oleander is a status effect that inflicts damage over time to afflicted Dragons. It acts similarly to the Wither Effect, except it acts slower, only harms Dragons, and scales in damage the longer the effect is active.

It can be cured by right clicking on an afflicted dragon with Scauldron Venom.


### Effect

When a Dragon initially aquires the Blue Oleander Effect, they take 4 damage (2 hearts) and then every 10 minutes there after while the effect is active, the dragon takes a damage tick from the effect. The damage from this tick increases by 50% compound each time it is applied, and this process continues until the dragon dies or the effect is removed.

Though the damage starts small, it can build up over time to the point where it cannot be offset by healing potions or the dragon's natural regeneration, and will eventually one-shot the dragon.

# Dragon Blood Scent

[TABLE]
Dragon Blood Scent (Effect)



Type | Negative
[/TABLE]

Dragon Blood Scent is a status effect that prevents an afflicted player from directly interacting with Dragons for the duration of the effect (typically 30 minutes).

The effect can not be removed with milk or commands. Dying or waiting for the effect to end will remove the effect.


### Effect

When a player is afflicted with the Dragon Blood Scent effect they cannot directly command, ride, or tame any Dragons for as long as the scent lingers.

Indirectly commanding Dragons with Dragon Command Items bypassess this effect.

[/TABLE]


# Dragon Nip

[TABLE]
Dragon Nip (Effect)



Type | Positive
[/TABLE]

This article is about the status effect. For the Block, see Dragon Nip Grass. For the Item, see Dragon Nip.

Dragon Nip is a status effect that makes affected Dragons passive for the effects duration & allows for taming wild Dragons.


### Effect

When a Dragon initially aquires the Dragon Nip Effect any current hostility is lost, and the dragon remains passive for the duration of the effect.

Additionally, while this effect is active on a wild dragon, it allows players to tame them without meeting the Dragons Taming Requirements (the correct food item is still required in its regular quantity).

This article is about the status effect. For the Block, see Wild Dragon Root. For the Item, see Dragon Root.

Dragon Root is a status effect that makes affected Dragons hostile towards other nearby dragons for the effects duration.

It can be cured by right clicking on an afflicted dragon with a Dragon Root Remedy.


### Effect

When a susceptible Dragon initially aquires the Dragon Root Effect they become hostile to any nearby dragons that are also susceptible to the effect, and the affected dragon remains hostile for the duration of the effect unless cured by right clicking on an afflicted dragon with a Dragon Root Remedy.

## Refined Dragon Root

[TABLE]
Refined Dragon Root (Effect)



Type | Negative

Immune Dragons | Crimson GoregutterGronckleHotburpleRumblehornScuttleclawThunderbottom
[/TABLE]

This article is about the status effect. For the Item, see Refined Dragon Root. For the arrows, see Refined Dragon Root Arrows

Dragon Root is a status effect that makes affected dragons passive for the effects duration, causes Dragons to fall out of the sky if in flight, has their movement crippled, and any riders will be kicked off.

It can be cured by right clicking on an afflicted dragon with a Dragon Root Remedy.


### Effect

When a Dragon is initially affected by the Refined Dragon Root Effect any current hostility is lost, and the dragon remains passive for the duration of the effect. If the Dragon is in flight, it will force the Dragon to land, and it will have limited movement for the duration of the effect. Any riders or passengers are forcibly dismounted from the affected dragon, and will be unable to remount the dragon until the effect has been removed.

Additionally, while this effect is active on a wild dragon, it allows players to feed them without meeting the Dragons Taming Requirements (the correct food item is still required in its regular quantity), but won't allow you to ride the dragon to finish taming them.
